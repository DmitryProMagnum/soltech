Every figure above is an illustrative model, not a quote or a record of a specific client. Actual savings turn on your roof orientation and shading, your load through the day, the exact DEWA-approved models specified and the tariff band you sit in — all confirmed at the assessment stage. A factory or warehouse tends to pay back fastest because it consumes most of its generation on site during daylight; a villa saves a smaller absolute sum but usually offsets a large share of its bill.

25-year view

The cost of ownership, over 25 years

Money leaves your pocket in both scenarios — the real question is how much. Below, a sample 15 kWp villa is modelled two ways across the working life of the panels: staying entirely on the DEWA grid, versus generating most of its own power.

Over 25 years Stay on DEWA Go solar · 15 kWp
Electricity paid to DEWA AED 300,000 AED 54,000
System investment — AED 52,500
Maintenance & one inverter replacement — AED 19,000
Net 25-year position −AED 300,000 −AED 125,500

On these assumptions the villa is roughly AED 174,500 better off over 25 years by going solar — and owns an asset that keeps producing rather than a bill that never stops.

Assumptions behind the model

  • System: ~15 kWp, generating roughly 26,250 kWh in year one (about 1,750 kWh per kWp) and easing by about 0.5% a year, for close to 615,000 kWh over 25 years.
  • Household use: about 30,000 kWh a year, so grid units the panels do not cover are still bought from DEWA.
  • Tariff: held flat at 0.40 AED per kWh — a deliberately conservative choice, since we apply no tariff inflation over the 25 years.
  • Ongoing cost: routine cleaning and monitoring, plus one inverter replacement partway through the system’s life.

Net metering does the accounting automatically: surplus daytime generation is credited at the retail rate and rolled forward, so the array works down the bill month after month. You can read how this works on our DEWA guide or on DEWA’s own Shams Dubai pages.

A note on every figure on this page. All savings, payback and 25-year numbers shown here are illustrative models, not quotes and not records of named clients. Your own figures depend on your roof, your consumption and the current DEWA tariff and equipment registry — and are confirmed only after a free engineering assessment.
