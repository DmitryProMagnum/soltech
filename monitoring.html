The data path

How the data reaches you

Your system produces two separate streams of data, and they travel different roads to different places. One settles your DEWA bill. The other shows you — and us — how the system is performing, minute by minute. They are independent: neither relies on the other, and knowing both is how you can trust that the numbers in front of you are real.

Stream A · DEWAThe metering stream — settles your bill

A bidirectional DEWA smart meter sits at your connection point and records energy in both directions — what you draw from the grid and what your system exports back. DEWA owns it, and installs or reprogrammes it after your project is approved, inspected and commissioned. It reports into DEWA’s own advanced metering infrastructure (AMI) automatically, over DEWA’s secure metering network, which you never touch or configure. Under Shams Dubai net metering, that reading is what your bill is settled on.

Stream B · MonitoringThe live stream — you and we watch it

Every SolTech inverter has communications built in: Wi-Fi, Ethernet, an optional 4G modem, and RS485 for add-on devices. Where an inverter has no built-in comms — common on larger or older commercial strings — a separate data logger reads it over RS485. Either way, readings upload every few minutes to the manufacturer’s cloud, which feeds the secure web portal for your system. Nothing here touches your DEWA bill; this stream exists so performance stays visible, and so a fault reaches a human.

The devices that do the talking

Inverter comms

Wi-Fi / Ethernet / 4G

Modern inverters ship with comms on board — Wi-Fi and Ethernet as standard, an optional 4G modem where the site has no reliable network. This is the inverter talking straight to the manufacturer’s cloud, with no extra hardware needed.

Wired bus

RS485

A short-range wired bus, and the long-standing standard for solar hardware. It carries readings between the inverter and add-on devices — a data logger, an energy meter, or a battery (ESS).

Gateway

Data logger

Where an inverter lacks built-in comms, a separate data logger polls it over RS485 and uploads to the cloud. On a multi-inverter site, one logger can gather the whole array into a single feed.

Panel level

Module-level optimisers

On SolarEdge systems, per-panel optimisers report each module separately — so a single underperforming panel shows up on its own rather than hiding in the string average.

What the inverter sends — every few minutes

  • Instantaneous power output (kW)
  • String voltage and current
  • Inverter temperature
  • Energy produced — today, this month, this year (kWh)
  • Inverter status and error codes
  • Battery / ESS state of charge, where fitted
  • On-site consumption and self-consumption
  • Energy exported to the grid

Questions

Monitoring, answered

Is it real-time?

Near-live rather than to-the-second. Your app refreshes typically every few minutes — indicative, and it varies by equipment model and settings; Huawei FusionSolar updates roughly every one to five minutes, SolarEdge closer to real-time. The data travels from the inverter, or a data logger over RS485, up to the manufacturer’s cloud and back down to your screen.

Do I need one app per brand?

No. Each manufacturer has its own platform, but you do not manage them. Where a vendor exposes an API, we pull the approved data into one SolTech view — subject to that vendor’s terms — so mixed-brand hardware still shows as a single dashboard.

Who sees my data?

Your generation data sits in the manufacturer’s secure cloud and in your SolTech dashboard. We watch it to run and maintain your system — that is the free fault monitoring — and we do not sell it. What we hold, and why, is set out in our privacy policy.

Does DEWA see the same numbers as my app?

They are two independent streams. Your app reads the inverter — what the system makes and where it flows. DEWA bills from its own bidirectional smart meter, which it installs and owns, measuring energy imported from and exported to the grid; that meter reports into DEWA’s own secure metering network automatically, and — depending on the meter model and DEWA’s configuration — the exact method and interval are not publicly disclosed. It is the meter, not your app, that Shams Dubai net metering settles your bill on, so small differences between the two are normal: they measure at different points.
