Maintenance & Monitoring

We keep your system earning — for 25 years

Installation is day one. What protects your return is what happens after: keeping the panels clean, catching faults the moment they appear, and holding your warranty. In Dubai’s dust and heat, that is the difference between a system that pays back and one that quietly under-earns.

Why it matters

A solar system is an asset. It needs to be maintained like one

Panels have no moving parts, which makes solar refreshingly low-maintenance — but “low” is not “none”, and in the Gulf two things quietly eat into output:

  • Dust and soiling. Field studies in the UAE found that panels left uncleaned for three months lost about 13% of their output, while a roughly fortnightly clean held the loss to around 4%. Neglected for longer, systems can shed 15–25%+. Every dusty week is generation you paid for and did not get.
  • Undetected faults. A tripped inverter, a disconnected string or a failing optimiser does not announce itself. Without monitoring it can waste 20–30% of output for weeks before anyone notices — usually when the DEWA bill creeps back up.

There is also the warranty. Manufacturer performance warranties — the 25-year promise that makes solar an investment — typically assume the system is monitored and maintained. Documented upkeep keeps those claims valid.

Maintenance protects your solar savings — it doesn’t replace them. Say the calculator shows your villa saving around AED 30,000 a year by going solar. Left alone, dust and undetected faults quietly take back AED 3,000–4,000 of that every year. A maintenance plan keeps most of it — so your system holds near its rated output, and your 25-year warranty stays valid, for a fraction of what it protects.

Questions

Maintenance, answered

How often do you clean the panels?

It depends on the plan and your location’s dust exposure. Monitor includes one clean a year, Performance a quarterly cycle, and Complete steps up to monthly cleaning through the dustiest months. Sites near sand, construction or heavy traffic are scheduled more often.

What happens if I just skip maintenance?

The system keeps working, but it slowly under-earns — dust alone can cost 10–15%+ a year — and an undetected fault can waste far more without anyone knowing. You may also weaken a future warranty claim. Nothing breaks overnight; you simply lose return quietly.

Is monitoring not already part of my system?

Your inverter records data, but someone has to watch it, interpret it and act. Our plans turn that raw data into alerts we respond to — monitoring is only useful if a fault actually reaches a human.

Do I need a plan to keep my warranty?

Not always, but manufacturer performance warranties generally expect the system to be maintained and monitored, and a documented record makes any claim far smoother. Our plans keep that record for you.

Can I change or cancel later?

Yes. Plans are annual and you can move up or down at renewal as your needs change. If you sell the property, the plan and its record can transfer with the system to the new owner.
