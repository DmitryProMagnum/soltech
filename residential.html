Why go solar

Your roof already has the sun. It may as well earn from it

Dubai gives a rooftop roughly 1,750 kWh a year for every kWp installed — among the best solar resources on earth. Owned outright, a home system turns that sunlight into three lasting advantages.

A much lower bill

Every kilowatt-hour your panels make is one you no longer buy from DEWA. Most villas offset 70–90% of their electricity, and net metering credits the surplus you export — so the meter runs backwards on a bright afternoon.

Energy on your own terms

You generate a large share of your own power on site, insulated from tariff bands and fuel surcharges creeping upward. Your cost per unit is fixed by an asset you own, not a rate someone else sets.

A more appealing home

A paid-off, professionally installed array with a live warranty is a genuine feature for buyers who care about running costs and sustainability. Any uplift is potential, not guaranteed — but a lower forever-bill is easy to appreciate.

What you get

A system you own — and a team that runs the hard parts

You buy the array (typically around AED 3–4 per watt installed) and it is yours from day one. Everything that makes that ownership painless — the DEWA process, the equipment choice, the warranty, the monitoring — sits with us.

01

Shams Dubai grid-tied

Your system is designed and filed under DEWA’s official Shams Dubai programme, so the power you don’t use is exported and credited back through net metering.

02

DEWA handled for you

We manage the whole DEWA process — application, approvals, the meter change and net-metering setup — end to end. You never chase a form or queue at a counter.

03

DEWA-eligible equipment

Only panels and inverters listed on DEWA’s Eligible Equipment registry, chosen for Dubai’s heat and a long working life — not the cheapest box on the shelf.

04

Long-term performance warranty

Your panels carry a long manufacturer performance warranty. We keep the maintenance record those warranties expect, so any claim stays valid and simple.

05

Monitoring for life

Free 24/7 fault monitoring on every system we install. If output dips or a string drops, we’re alerted — usually before you’d ever notice it on your bill.

06

You own the asset

The array belongs to you outright — no lease, no monthly service fee for the power. Once it has paid for itself, its generation is effectively free for the rest of its life.

Questions

Home solar, answered

What does a home system cost, and do I actually own it?

Yes — on the residential track you buy the system outright, so it’s yours from the day it’s switched on. As a rough guide, installed cost runs around AED 3–4 per watt, so the total depends on how large an array your roof and usage justify. There’s no lease and no monthly fee for the power it makes; after payback, the generation is effectively free.

How long until it pays for itself?

For a well-sited villa, payback typically lands in five to seven years, after which the system keeps producing for many more years — largely free electricity for the rest of its working life. The exact payback depends on your bill, roof and shading, which is what the free assessment confirms.

Will solar keep my lights on during a power cut?

Not on its own. A standard grid-tied Shams Dubai system is designed to work with the grid, and for safety the inverter shuts down during a DEWA outage — so panels alone do not provide backup. If keeping power through outages matters to you, that needs a battery added to the system, which we can size and quote separately.

What happens if I sell the villa?

The system stays with the property and transfers to the new owner, along with its warranty and monitoring record. A paid-off array that cuts the running cost of the home is a real selling point — though any effect on sale price is potential rather than a fixed figure we’d ever promise.
