Straight Answers

Questions people actually ask

A grid-tied system replaces the power you buy from DEWA at 0.38–0.46 AED/kWh with your own at roughly 0.11 AED/kWh. For most villas that removes 70–90% of the electricity bill, and the system typically pays for itself in about 5–7 years — leaving 18–20 more years of near-free power under a 25-year panel performance warranty. Enter your monthly bill in the calculator above for a figure tuned to your home; final numbers are confirmed by the free engineering assessment.

Yes. The system is part of the property and transfers to the buyer, who inherits 20+ years of electricity at a fraction of the grid price — an increasingly clear selling point in Dubai. Accumulated DEWA bill credits stay with the account and do not transfer.

We do, end to end. SolTech designs the system, files it under DEWA's Shams Dubai programme, and manages the connection approval — using only panels and inverters from DEWA's Eligible Equipment list. You never deal with the paperwork.

A typical villa goes from first calculation to switch-on in 45–60 days. Most of that is DEWA approval time; the physical installation on your roof takes only 2–7 days.

Yes. Every roof gets a structural assessment first, the inverters are DEWA-listed and shut down automatically if the grid goes down (a built-in safety requirement), and installation is handled by certified engineers. With no moving parts, there is very little that can go wrong.
