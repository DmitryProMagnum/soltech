Methodology

How we calculate

The estimate is deliberately conservative — we would rather under-promise. It is a pre-assessment model, not a quote. Here is exactly what sits behind the numbers:

  • Your consumption is inferred from your monthly bill using an all-in DEWA cost per kWh — about 0.40 AED/kWh for a villa and 0.44 AED/kWh for a business (slab tariff plus the fuel surcharge and VAT).
  • System size is set to offset roughly 85% of your annual consumption, at a Dubai yield of ~1,750 kWh per kWp per year — a cautious figure for real rooftop conditions, heat and soiling included.
  • Investment uses an installed cost of about 3,800 AED/kWp for villas and 2,800 AED/kWp for larger business systems.
  • Savings assume net metering credits each offset kWh at the retail rate. Self-generated solar works out around 0.11 AED/kWh over its life.
  • 25-year net savings apply a 0.92 average output factor for panel degradation and assume no electricity-price inflation — so the real figure is likely higher. The system investment is already deducted.
  • CO₂ avoided uses the UAE grid factor of ~0.40 kg CO₂ per kWh.

Your roof orientation, shading, structure and actual consumption pattern all move these numbers. That is what the free engineering assessment is for. See the DEWA & Shams Dubai guide for how net metering and approvals work.

All figures are illustrative models, not quotes or guarantees. Final numbers follow a free engineering assessment of your property.
